# Evidence Checklist: 2025 Jeep Grand Cherokee (Chrysler Capital / Bank of America)

**Account:** Bank of America Auto Loan  
**Collateral:** 2025 Jeep Grand Cherokee  
**Purpose:** Litigation readiness & standing verification  
**Legal Basis:** FDCPA 15 USC §1692g, UCC §3-301, O.C.G.A. §9-3-24  

---

## Phase 1: Core Loan Documents (CRITICAL)

### ✅ Required Documents to Request

1. **Original Promissory Note (Wet-Ink)**
   - **Why:** UCC §3-301 requires "person entitled to enforce" must have possession
   - **Red Flags:** Robo-signed, missing signature, stamped signature, copy instead of original
   - **Storage:** Upload to Evidence tab in R.O.M.A.N. → 2025 Jeep folder

2. **Retail Installment Sales Contract (RISC)**
   - **Why:** Contains all terms, APR, total cost - TILA compliance check
   - **Red Flags:** Blank fields at signing, altered terms, missing disclosures
   - **Storage:** Contract Analyzer tab for AI flaw detection

3. **Truth in Lending Disclosure (TILA)**
   - **Why:** Required by 15 USC §1638 - violations = rescission rights
   - **Red Flags:** Wrong APR calculation, missing finance charge disclosure
   - **Storage:** Evidence tab + FDCPA tracker for violations

4. **Complete Payment History**
   - **Why:** Statute of limitations calculation, overcharge detection
   - **Red Flags:** Unexplained fees, incorrect interest calculations
   - **Storage:** Business Debt tracker for payment verification

---

## Phase 2: Chain of Title Documents (STANDING CHALLENGE)

### ✅ Assignment Records

5. **Assignment of Security Interest**
   - **Why:** Proves who actually owns the right to collect
   - **Red Flags:** Missing assignment, backdated, unsigned, breaks in chain
   - **Legal Standard:** UCC §3-301 - must prove unbroken chain from dealer → Chrysler → BofA
   - **Storage:** Evidence tab - critical for Motion to Dismiss if sued

6. **Power of Attorney / Servicing Agreement**
   - **Why:** If BofA is just a "servicer" not owner, they lack standing
   - **Red Flags:** Servicing agreement doesn't grant collection authority
   - **Storage:** Contract Analyzer for authority verification

7. **Securitization Trust Documents (if applicable)**
   - **Why:** If loan was securitized, trust may be real party in interest
   - **Red Flags:** Pooling & Servicing Agreement (PSA) deadline missed = void transfer
   - **Note:** This is EVIDENCE of broken chain of title, NOT a "securitization surplus" claim
   - **Storage:** Evidence tab for standing challenge support

---

## Phase 3: Compliance Documents (FDCPA/FCRA VIOLATIONS)

### ✅ Consumer Protection Records

8. **Initial Collection Letter**
   - **Why:** Must contain "Mini-Miranda" warning per FDCPA
   - **Red Flags:** Missing validation notice, threat of illegal action
   - **Storage:** FDCPA tracker → $1,000 statutory damages if violated

9. **Credit Bureau Reporting Records**
   - **Why:** FCRA requires accurate reporting
   - **Red Flags:** Incorrect balance, wrong dates, failure to update after dispute
   - **Storage:** Active Credit tab for FCRA violation tracking

10. **All Written Communications**
    - **Why:** Pattern of harassment = FDCPA violations
    - **Red Flags:** Calls before 8am/after 9pm, threatening language, false urgency
    - **Storage:** Evidence tab - builds counterclaim case

---

## Phase 4: Defense-Specific Documents

### ✅ Affirmative Defense Support

11. **Payoff Statement Request (Current)**
    - **Why:** Compare to contract terms for overcharges
    - **Red Flags:** Fees not in original contract, wrong interest calculation
    - **Storage:** Payment Plan Calculator for settlement analysis

12. **Insurance Policy (GAP Insurance if purchased)**
    - **Why:** May cover negative equity or total loss
    - **Red Flags:** Policy never activated, claims denied improperly
    - **Storage:** Insurance Gap Tracker

13. **Vehicle Inspection Report (at purchase)**
    - **Why:** If undisclosed defects, dealer fraud claim
    - **Red Flags:** Salvage title not disclosed, odometer rollback
    - **Storage:** Evidence tab for potential fraud counterclaim

## Document Request Letter Template

**Use this when requesting from Bank of America:**

```
[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[Date]

Bank of America Auto Loans
Attn: Loan Servicing Department
[Address]

Re: Account #[Last 4 digits]  
    2025 Jeep Grand Cherokee  
    Debt Verification Request (15 USC §1692g)

Dear Sir/Madam:

This is a formal request for verification of the above-referenced debt pursuant to the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act, 15 USC §1692g.

Please provide the following documents within 30 days:

1. Complete copy of the original promissory note bearing my wet-ink signature
2. Complete chain of title showing assignment from [Dealer] to Chrysler Capital to Bank of America
3. Itemized payment history from inception to present
4. Truth in Lending Disclosure (TILA) statement
5. Documentation proving your authority to collect on this account

Until such verification is provided, I request that:
- All collection activity cease
- No negative information be reported to credit bureaus
- No legal action be initiated

This letter is sent via USPS Certified Mail for proof of delivery.

Sincerely,
[Signature]
[Name]
```

**Send via:** USPS Certified Mail, Return Receipt Requested (~$8)  
**Tracking:** Upload tracking number to Legal Defense Dashboard → Deadlines tab

## What Success Looks Like

### Scenario 1: They Provide Everything
- Documents are clean → Use Payment Plan Calculator to negotiate best payoff terms
- Your insurance may cover some costs → Insurance Gap Tracker shows coverage

### Scenario 2: They Can't Prove Standing
- Missing assignment → File Motion to Dismiss if sued
- Robo-signed note → Contract Analyzer flags it → Affirmative defense

### Scenario 3: FDCPA Violations Found
- Missing Mini-Miranda → Counterclaim for $1,000 statutory damages
- Harassment documented → Additional $1,000 per violation + attorney fees

## Legal Limits (Stay in Bounds)

✅ **This is legitimate:** Requesting documents, tracking violations, preserving evidence  
❌ **This is NOT:** Claiming debts are "discharged" by requesting documents  

**The goal:** Build a complete evidentiary record so IF you're sued, you have affirmative defenses ready. Or IF they violated FDCPA, you have a counterclaim.

**NOT the goal:** Trying to make the debt "disappear" via document requests alone.
