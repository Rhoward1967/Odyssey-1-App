## RETENTION REQUIREMENTS

**Federal Law (IRS):**
- Keep all business records for **7 years** from date of tax return filing
- Keep employment records (W-2s) for **4 years** after tax due date
- Keep contractor records (1099s) for **4 years** after tax due date

**Georgia State Law:**
- Business license records: **Permanent** (as long as business operates)
- Sales tax records: **4 years** from date of filing
- Workers' compensation: **5 years** after coverage ends

**Best Practice:** Keep all records permanently (storage is cheap, litigation is expensive)

---

## ANNUAL ARCHIVING PROCESS

**At end of each calendar year:**
1. Print final versions of all operational documents
2. Create "YEAR_2026_ARCHIVE" folder (update year annually)
3. Move to long-term storage (physical or digital)
4. Start fresh operational logs for new year

**Do NOT destroy old archives.** Even 10-year-old contractor agreements can be relevant in patent litigation.
