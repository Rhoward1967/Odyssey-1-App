### 1.2 Description of Related Art

Current humanoid robotic chassis systems and wearable exoskeleton designs suffer from critical architectural limitations:

**Centralized Spine Architecture (Dominant Humanoid Robotics):**
All major commercial humanoid robots (Boston Dynamics Atlas, Tesla Optimus, Agility Robotics Digit) use a central rigid spine with limbs radiating outward. This architecture creates:
- Single-point-of-failure spine — damage to central structure disables entire system
- Non-modular limb attachment — replacement requires structural disassembly
- Component obsolescence — upgrading processing or power requires replacing entire subsystems
- No geometric invariance — arm, torso, and leg cells have different structural ratios, preventing cross-region part interchangeability
- Fixed-scale design — humanoid scale is not fractally related to exoskeleton or micro-robot scale

**Servo/Pneumatic Actuation Systems:**
Current humanoid locomotion uses rotational servo motors or pneumatic actuators:
- Servo motors: mechanical gearbox failure points, high acoustic signature, limited force-to-weight ratio
- Pneumatic actuators: require compressor infrastructure, slow response, cannot derive power from onboard harmonic power source
- Neither system is frequency-coherent with the power source's electromagnetic operating characteristics
- No energy recapture from actuation — all output energy is lost to friction and heat

**Modular Robotics (Prior Art — HEBI Robotics, Modular Robotics):**
Existing modular robotic systems use standardized joint modules but suffer from:
- Non-geometric module arrangement (no IVM or fractal geometry — modules are interchangeable but not geometrically invariant)
- Wired interconnects — copper wiring harness is the primary failure point in field-deployed modular robots
- No optical data transmission between cells
- No structural power integration — batteries remain separate payload, not structural members
- No self-healing outer layer

**Exoskeleton Systems (ReWalk, Ekso, Sarcos):**
Current wearable exoskeletons:
- Use separate structural and power subsystems (frame + battery pack)
- Require custom fabrication for different users
- Cannot scale to micro-robot configuration using same geometry
- No cell replacement without structural disassembly
- No fractal relationship to companion autonomous platform

**None of the prior art addresses:** a single geometric lattice architecture that simultaneously governs structural integrity at humanoid scale, interfaces with the human body at exoskeleton scale, and scales to sub-millimeter micro-robot configurations — while providing tool-free cell replacement, optical inter-cell communication, bio-synthetic actuation powered by the chassis's own resonant power system, and structural mass co-located with functional (power/compute) mass.
