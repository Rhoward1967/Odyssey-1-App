## THE WORD

Begin with a word. One word that contains the entire history of what this book is about.

**E M A N C I P A T I O N**

Say it slowly. Break it open.

**E-** (ex: out of) — **MAN-** (manus: the Hand) — **CIP-** (capere: to seize) — **-ATIO** (the act of doing)

*The act of being taken out of the seizing hand.*

That is what the word means. Not freedom granted. Not liberation given. The act of being taken out of a hand that was seizing you. The hand is still the reference point. The hand is still the center of the legal universe even in the act that was supposed to end its authority over you.

In Roman law, manus was not a metaphor. It was a specific legal institution — the formal power held by the paterfamilias over his wife, his children, and his slaves. To be in manu was to be in the hand: property with no independent legal standing, no ability to own, no voice before any court except through the one whose hand held you.

The American slave system imported this structure. Not just the practice of bondage but the specific legal architecture of the hand's ownership. The admiralty courts that classified enslaved persons as maritime cargo. The insurance documents that valued them as commercial property. The constitutional provisions that counted them as three-fifths of a person while denying them any rights whatsoever.

When formal emancipation came, the living being was taken out of the seizing hand. E-manus-capere. But the hand remained. The Black Codes came within eighteen days of ratification of the Thirteenth Amendment. The Slaughterhouse Cases gutted the Fourteenth Amendment within five years. The convict leasing system re-enslaved through the punishment exception. The redlining maps drew new lines of exclusion across every American city.

*They were sorry for what was done. But here, try this chain on. It's a little lighter to carry.*

This book is about the chain. Not the lighter version. The mechanism. The hand. And what the living being does when they understand — really understand, in the law's own language, with the law's own evidence — that the hand that seized them never had the right to close.
