### ✅ **STEP 5: Get Documents Notarized**

**Which Documents:**
1. ✅ **Sovereignty Declaration** (MUST notarize - both signature pages)
2. ✅ **ICC Complaint** (SHOULD notarize - signature page)
3. ✅ **UN HRC Petition** (SHOULD notarize - signature page)

**Notary Options:**

**Option A: U.S. Notary (Acceptable, Easiest)**
- Find local notary:
  - UPS Store
  - Banks (if you're customer)
  - Athens City Hall / Clarke County Courthouse
  - Mobile notary (search "notary Athens GA")

- **Cost:** $2-$10 per signature
- **What to Bring:**
  - Government-issued ID (driver's license, passport)
  - Unsigned documents (sign in front of notary)

- **Notary Should Write:**
  - "Sworn to and subscribed before me this ___ day of _______, 2026"
  - Notary signature, seal, commission expiration

**Option B: U.S. Notary + Apostille (BETTER for international)**
1. Get notarized by U.S. notary (above)
2. Send to Georgia Secretary of State for Apostille:
   ```
   Georgia Secretary of State
   Authentications Unit
   2 Martin Luther King Jr. Dr SE, Suite 313
   Atlanta, GA 30334
   Phone: (404) 656-2881
   ```
3. Request: "Apostille for international use"
4. Include:
   - Notarized documents
   - Check/money order ($5 per document)
   - Cover letter explaining purpose
5. Processing: 3-5 business days
6. **Apostille:** Special certificate from GA Secretary of State that international bodies recognize

**Option C: International Notary (BEST, but harder to find)**
- Specialized notary for international documents
- May be in Atlanta (call Ethiopian consulate, they may know)
- Most credible for ICC/UN
- **Cost:** $50-$200

**After Notarization:**
- [ ] Check notary used correct date
- [ ] Check seal is clear and legible
- [ ] Make copies BEFORE mailing (notarized originals go to ICC/UN)
