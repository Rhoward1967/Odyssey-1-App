## COMPLAINT FOR DAMAGES AND INJUNCTIVE RELIEF

---

### INTRODUCTION

Plaintiff Rickey Allan Howard brings this action against Bank of America, N.A. for willful violations of the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), the Truth in Lending Act (TILA), and the Georgia Fair Business Practices Act. Bank of America received four separate certified mail notices over a period of three months, signed for each by its own employees, and failed to respond to any of them — blowing through three separate statutory deadlines. This is not a case of inadvertence. This is a documented pattern of willful non-response to legally required consumer protection obligations.

---

### PARTIES

**1.** Plaintiff Rickey Allan Howard is a natural person domiciled in Clarke County, Georgia, at 595 Macon Hwy, Apt 35, Athens, Georgia 30606. Plaintiff is a "consumer" as defined by 15 U.S.C. § 1681a(c).

**2.** Plaintiff Howard Jones Bloodline Ancestral Trust (EIN: 41-6850149) is an irrevocable trust organized under the laws of the State of Georgia, with its administrative record filed in Clarke County, Georgia (Public Record Filing ID: 15152515). Rickey Allan Howard is the Grantor-Trustee.

**3.** Defendant Bank of America, N.A. is a national banking association with its principal place of business at 100 N. Tryon Street, Charlotte, NC 28255. Bank of America is a "furnisher of information" as defined under 15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2 and regularly furnishes consumer credit information to Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. Bank of America may be served through its Georgia registered agent: CT Corporation System, 289 S. Culver Street, Lawrenceville, GA 30046.

---

### JURISDICTION AND VENUE

**4.** This Court has subject matter jurisdiction over Plaintiff's Georgia state law claims under O.C.G.A. § 10-1-393 and concurrent jurisdiction over federal claims under 15 U.S.C. § 1681p (FCRA) and 15 U.S.C. § 1640(e) (TILA).

**5.** Venue is proper in Clarke County, Georgia under O.C.G.A. § 9-10-93 because Plaintiff resides in Clarke County, the harm was suffered in Clarke County, and Plaintiff's public record filings establishing constructive notice are recorded in Clarke County.

**6.** This Court has personal jurisdiction over Bank of America, N.A. under Georgia's long-arm statute, O.C.G.A. § 9-10-91, because Bank of America regularly conducts business with Georgia residents and committed tortious acts against a Georgia resident within this state.

### FACTUAL BACKGROUND

**7.** On or about April 30, 2024, Plaintiff entered into an auto loan agreement with Bank of America, N.A. for the purchase of a 2024 Jeep Wrangler, Account Number 63010066944180, with a principal balance of approximately $49,333.47.

**8.** Bank of America is currently reporting information relating to Account 63010066944180 to consumer reporting agencies including Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion.

**9.** Bank of America is listed as Loss Payee on Plaintiff's commercial vehicle insurance policy (GEICO Policy No. 9300324264, effective May 6, 2026), confirming its ongoing assertion of a lien interest in the vehicle while simultaneously refusing to validate the debt or produce the required loan documentation.

---

This mailing was delivered to 100 N. Tryon Street, Charlotte, NC 28255, confirmed by USPS electronic scan and signed green card. **Article Number: 9589 0710 5270 2244 1851 51. **12.** A second certified mailing was delivered to Bank of America's El Paso, TX servicing center on approximately February 18, 2026 (Tracking: 9589071052702244185069), delivered to an individual in Lawrenceville, GA 30043. Bank of America provided no response.

Plaintiff simultaneously filed formal complaints with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) and the Georgia Attorney General Consumer Protection Division against Bank of America.

**14.** The CFPB complaint was filed on March 12, 2026 citing TILA and FCRA violations on Account 63010066944180. The Georgia Attorney General complaint was filed on March 12, 2026 citing TILA, FCRA, and O.C.G.A. § 10-1-393. The Georgia AG complaint was received and signed by **C.A. Tillman** on March 20, 2026.

**15.** A subsequent certified mailing was delivered to Bank of America's El Paso address on March 17–18, 2026 (Tracking: 9589071052702244169922). Bank of America provided no response.

**16.** On April 9, 2026, Plaintiff served Bank of America with a Notice of Continued Default — FCRA Willful Violation, via USPS Certified Mail (Article: 9589 0710 5270 2244 1851 51, delivered approximately April 11, 2026). This Notice formally cited 15 U.S.C. § 1681n willful violation, noted that 25 days had elapsed past the March 10, 2026 statutory deadline, and issued a final demand requiring written response within fifteen (15) days — no later than **May 9, 2026** — or Plaintiff would immediately file civil action.

**17.** As of May 10, 2026, Bank of America has provided **zero response** to any of the four certified mailings spanning February 4, 2026 through April 9, 2026. Three separate statutory response deadlines have elapsed — March 10, April 17, and May 9, 2026 — with complete silence from Bank of America on each.

**18.** Bank of America has continued to furnish information regarding Account 63010066944180 to consumer reporting agencies throughout this period without notation that the account is formally disputed.

---

### COUNT I
**Willful Violation of 15 U.S.C. § 1681n**
**Fair Credit Reporting Act — Failure to Respond to Formal Dispute**

**19.** Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

**20.** Under 15 U.S.C. § 1681i(a)(1), upon receipt of a formal written dispute, Bank of America was required to conduct a reasonable reinvestigation and provide written response within thirty (30) days of confirmed delivery.

**21.** Bank of America received Plaintiff's formal written dispute on or about February 4–18, 2026. Three separate response deadlines — March 10, April 17, and May 9, 2026 — have elapsed with no response.

**22.** Bank of America's failure to respond was willful within the meaning of 15 U.S.C. § 1681n, entitling Plaintiff to statutory damages of $100 to $1,000 per violation, actual damages, punitive damages, and attorney fees.

---

### COUNT II
**Willful Violation of 15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2(b)**
**Failure to Investigate and Correct Disputed Information as Furnisher**

**23.** Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

**24.** As a furnisher of information under 15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2, Bank of America was required upon receipt of notice of a consumer dispute to: (a) conduct a reasonable investigation; (b) review all relevant information; (c) report the results to the credit reporting agencies; and (d) modify, delete, or permanently block reporting of inaccurate information.

**25.** Bank of America failed to take any of these required actions. Bank of America has continued to report Account 63010066944180 to consumer reporting agencies without the required "Disputed by Consumer" notation, in willful violation of 15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2(b).

**26.** This willful violation entitles Plaintiff to statutory damages, actual damages, and attorney fees under 15 U.S.C. § 1681n.

---

### COUNT III
**Violation of 15 U.S.C. § 1601 et seq.**
**Truth in Lending Act — Failure to Produce Loan Documentation**

**27.** Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

**28.** Plaintiff's February 4, 2026 Notice included a formal demand for: (a) a certified copy of the original retail installment contract bearing Plaintiff's wet ink or electronic signature; and (b) the Certificate of Title reflecting the exact lien holder entry — both required disclosures under TILA.

**29.** Bank of America received this demand, signed for it, and failed to produce any of the requested documentation within the statutory period or at any time thereafter.

**30.** Bank of America's failure to respond to a TILA documentation request while continuing to assert a lien and report the account to consumer reporting agencies constitutes a violation of 15 U.S.C. § 1601 et seq., exposing Bank of America to civil liability under 15 U.S.C. § 1640(a) including actual damages and statutory damages up to $4,000.

---

### COUNT IV
**Violation of O.C.G.A. § 10-1-393**
**Georgia Fair Business Practices Act — Unfair and Deceptive Acts and Practices**

**31.** Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

**32.** Under O.C.G.A. § 10-1-393, it is unlawful for any business to engage in unfair or deceptive acts or practices in the conduct of consumer transactions in Georgia.

**33.** Bank of America's conduct — accepting certified mail, having its employees sign for each delivery, and then maintaining complete silence across three statutory deadlines while continuing to report an unvalidated account as a delinquent debt to consumer reporting agencies affecting a Georgia resident — constitutes an unfair and deceptive business practice under O.C.G.A. § 10-1-393.

**34.** Bank of America's conduct was willful within the meaning of O.C.G.A. § 10-1-399, entitling Plaintiff to actual damages, treble damages, and attorney fees.

---

### COUNT V
**Defamation — Libel Per Se**

**35.** Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

**36.** Bank of America has published information to Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion characterizing Account 63010066944180 as a delinquent obligation — after receiving formal written notice of dispute, and after Bank of America's own employees signed for that notice.

**37.** The publication of false or unvalidated credit information to consumer reporting agencies without the required dispute notation, after receipt of formal written dispute, constitutes libel per se under Georgia law, directly injuring Plaintiff in his trade, business, and financial reputation.

**38.** Bank of America acted with actual malice — at minimum reckless disregard for the truth — by continuing adverse reporting after receiving four certified notices over three months.

**39.** Plaintiff is entitled to actual damages, presumed damages, and punitive damages.

---
