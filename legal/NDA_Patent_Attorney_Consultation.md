## 1. PURPOSE

The Disclosing Party is seeking legal counsel regarding the conversion of United
States Provisional Patent Application No. **63/913,134** ("R.O.M.A.N. 2.0
Dual-Hemisphere Artificial Intelligence System") to a nonprovisional utility patent
under 35 U.S.C. § 111(a), with a conversion deadline of **November 7, 2026**.

In connection with this consultation, the Disclosing Party will share confidential
technical and legal information with the Receiving Party. This Agreement governs
the protection of that information before and during the consultation, and prior
to the execution of a formal engagement letter.

---

## 2. CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

"Confidential Information" means all technical, legal, business, and financial
information disclosed by the Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party in connection
with this consultation, including but not limited to:

- The 22 innovations disclosed in Application No. 63/913,134
- Hardware architecture specifications (R.O.M.A.N. SoC, Lumen Core 3×3×3,
  Locus Ring, Vapor-Chamber Cooling, Photovoltaic Power Grid, Lumen-Link Protocol)
- Software architecture specifications (Dual-Hemisphere AI, Single Source of Truth,
  Synchronization Layer, HiveOrchestrator, and all related sovereign-core components)
- The Odyssey-1 codebase and any source files, documents, or diagrams shared
- The identity, valuation, and ownership structure of the Howard Jones Bloodline
  Ancestral Trust's intellectual property portfolio
- All documents in the `legal/` directory of the Odyssey-1 system shared during
  or in connection with the consultation
- Any oral disclosures made during the consultation

---

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

The Receiving Party agrees to:

a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence using at least the same
   degree of care used to protect its own confidential information, but no less
   than reasonable care;

b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior
   written consent of the Disclosing Party;

c) Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of evaluating and potentially
   providing legal services to the Disclosing Party in connection with USPTO
   Application No. 63/913,134;

d) Not file, register, or claim any interest in any invention, design, or work
   disclosed by the Disclosing Party;

e) Promptly notify the Disclosing Party upon discovery of any unauthorized
   disclosure or use of Confidential Information.

---

## 4. EXCEPTIONS

Obligations under Section 3 do not apply to information that:

a) Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement;

b) Was already known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure, as evidenced
   by written records predating this Agreement;

c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of the
   Confidential Information;

d) Is required to be disclosed by court order or law, provided the Receiving
   Party gives prompt written notice to the Disclosing Party before disclosure
   and cooperates with any effort to obtain a protective order.

---

## 5. OWNERSHIP

All Confidential Information remains the exclusive property of the Howard Jones
Bloodline Ancestral Trust. Nothing in this Agreement grants the Receiving Party
any license, right, or interest in any patent, copyright, trade secret, or other
intellectual property of the Disclosing Party.

Disclosure of Confidential Information does not constitute a public disclosure
for purposes of 35 U.S.C. § 102 or any foreign patent law.

---

## 6. TERM

This Agreement is effective from the date signed and continues for **three (3) years**,
or until the Disclosing Party provides written notice of termination, whichever
is later.

Upon termination or at the Disclosing Party's written request, the Receiving Party
shall promptly return or destroy all documents, files, and materials containing
Confidential Information.

---

## 7. NO ENGAGEMENT

This Agreement does not constitute a legal engagement, retainer, or attorney-client
relationship. A formal engagement letter, executed separately, is required to
establish the attorney-client relationship and the full protections of attorney-client
privilege.
