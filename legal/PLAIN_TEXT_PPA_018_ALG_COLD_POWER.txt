ABSTRACT
================================================================

An aluminum-graphene electrochemical cell (Al-G cell) wherein the electrode
geometry is arranged as a continuous toroidal manifold rather than the
conventional planar or cylindrical architecture. The internal electromagnetic
field generated during charge and discharge cycles induces a Lorentz force on
the ionic electrolyte, circulating it in a laminar donut-shaped flow pattern
that prevents thermal hot spot formation without external cooling systems. Ion
transport is accelerated by a three-dimensional graphene spongiform scaffold
with simultaneous center-and-periphery ingress pathways, reducing effective ion
travel distance to the molecular scale and enabling 0-100% charge in under 60
seconds without lithium plating or electrolyte decomposition. Charging current
is pulsed at 7.83 Hz (Schumann Resonance) to achieve harmonic entrainment of
aluminum ion oscillation, minimizing internal impedance and maximizing charge
acceptance efficiency. In the integrated embodiment, an external Harmonic
Resonance Grid (HRG) broadcasting a 7.83 Hz ambient electromagnetic field
serves as the carrier wave for the cell's charge management system, creating
a resonant cavity power architecture. The system operates at ambient temperature
under high-C loads, uses no lithium, cobalt, nickel, or rare earth elements,
and is designed in a modular blade form factor that functions as a structural
chassis element in wearable and autonomous platform applications.

1. BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
================================================================

1.1 FIELD OF THE INVENTION

This invention relates to electrochemical energy storage systems, specifically
to aluminum-graphene battery architectures utilizing toroidal electrode
geometry, Lorentz-force electrolyte circulation, three-dimensional
flash-intercalation scaffolding, and Schumann Resonance harmonic charge
management for high-power-density, zero-thermal-runaway energy storage in
wearable defense systems and autonomous AI hardware platforms.

1.2 DESCRIPTION OF RELATED ART

Lithium-Ion Architecture (Dominant Technology): Current lithium-ion cells use
a planar "ping-pong" ion transport model between flat cathode and anode sheets.
This creates linear ion travel paths with high tortuosity (winding distance
increases resistance and heat), hot spot formation at electrode boundaries
during high-C charge/discharge, thermal runaway risk above 150 degrees C,
cycle life degradation from lithium plating during fast charging, geopolitical
supply chain dependency (lithium from Chile/Argentina, cobalt from Democratic
Republic of Congo), and maximum charge rate limited to 1-4C requiring 15-60
minutes for full charge.

Conventional Aluminum-Ion Research: The 2015 Stanford University
aluminum-graphene battery demonstrated Al3+ intercalation into graphite foam
cathodes with 7,500 cycle life. Subsequent work by GMG (Graphene Manufacturing
Group, Brisbane, Australia, 2021-2025) demonstrated commercial aluminum-ion
cells with 60-second charging. However, all prior aluminum-ion art retains
linear electrode architecture (ion path still exhibits tortuosity), uses only
passive thermal management, does not teach electromagnetic circulation of
electrolyte by Lorentz-force method, uses only single-path ion ingress, and
uses no frequency-domain charge management.

None of the prior art addresses: a toroidal electrode geometry generating
active Lorentz-force electrolyte circulation that eliminates hot spots, combined
with dual-path three-dimensional ion ingress that eliminates tortuosity, and
harmonic resonance charging that synchronizes ion oscillation for maximum charge
acceptance -- all in a lithium-free, rare-earth-free system.

1.3 OBJECTS AND ADVANTAGES

1. Zero Thermal Runaway: Lorentz-force torus circulation prevents hot spot
formation at any charge rate; operating temperature remains at ambient even
under 60C loads.

2. 60-Second Full Charge: 3D spongiform scaffold with dual-path ion ingress
reduces ion travel distance to single graphene layer thickness across full
electrode surface area.

3. Harmonic Impedance Minimization: 7.83 Hz Schumann pulse synchronizes Al3+

4. HRG Ecosystem Integration: External 7.83 Hz ambient field serves as carrier
wave, extending harmonic benefits to entire wearable system.

5. Structural Dual-Function: Blade form factor allows battery cells to serve
as load-bearing chassis elements, reducing total system weight.

6. Sustainable Supply Chain: Aluminum (third most abundant element in Earth's
crust) plus graphene (carbon, abundant); zero lithium, cobalt, nickel, or rare
earth requirements.

7. Cycle Life: Toroidal architecture eliminates electrode boundary stress;
projected more than 20,000 cycles without capacity degradation.

3. DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF THE PREFERRED EMBODIMENT
================================================================

3.1 THE TOROIDAL CATHODE ARCHITECTURE

Substrate Geometry: The cathode is deposited on a toroidal substrate with
major radius R (outer center to torus center) and minor radius r (tube radius).
Preferred dimensions for wearable blade cell: R = 25mm, r = 8mm, producing
a cell with 33mm outer diameter and 17mm inner hollow.

Electromagnetic Field Generation: During charge/discharge, current flowing
through the toroidal cathode generates a toroidal magnetic field (B_t) inside
the torus tube and a poloidal magnetic field (B_p) around the torus tube.
The combined field geometry creates the Lorentz force conditions for
electrolyte circulation.

Cathode Material: Aluminum-graphene composite. Graphene base: CVD-grown
multi-layer graphene (3-7 layers). Aluminum deposition: Physical vapor
deposition of aluminum nanoparticles (20-50nm) onto graphene surface.
Composite thickness: 2-5 micrometers per layer, 10-20 layers stacked.
Intercalation capacity: 70-110 mAh/g (graphene) plus 20-30 mAh/g (aluminum
sites).

Anode Material: Aluminum metal foil (99.99% purity), formed to follow the
inner surface of the torus hollow.

3.2 THE LORENTZ-FORCE ELECTROLYTE CIRCULATION SYSTEM

Electrolyte Composition (Preferred):
- Aluminum chloride (AlCl3): 1.3-1.5 molar ratio
- 1-Ethyl-3-methylimidazolium chloride ([EMIm]Cl): ionic liquid carrier
- Total ionic conductivity: 8-12 mS/cm at 25 degrees C
- Viscosity: 15-25 mPa-s at 25 degrees C

Lorentz Force Calculation: For electrolyte current density J flowing in the
toroidal field B, F = J x B (vector cross product). The toroidal geometry
ensures F is always tangential to the torus tube cross-section, inducing
laminar rotational flow. For typical operating parameters (J = 500 A/m2,
B_t = 0.05 T), F = 25 N/m3 -- sufficient to maintain continuous circulation
at 5-15 mm/s fluid velocity.

Thermal Distribution: With continuous laminar circulation at 5-15 mm/s,
thermal energy deposited at any electrode surface point is distributed around
the full torus circumference within 2-4 seconds. Combined with passive graphene
thermal interface layer (5000 W/mK), operating temperature remains within 5
degrees C of ambient at charge rates up to 60C.

No Moving Parts: The Lorentz-force circulation is purely electromagnetic.
No pump, fan, or thermal management subsystem is required.

3.3 THE 3D GRAPHENE SPONGIFORM FLASH-INTERCALATION SCAFFOLD

Scaffold Architecture: Three-dimensional graphene foam fabricated by CVD growth
on nickel foam template followed by template removal, producing an open-cell
structure with:
- Macropore diameter: 1-10 micrometers
- Graphene wall thickness: 0.34-1.02 nm (1-3 graphene layers)
- Porosity: 85-95%
- Specific surface area: 500-1000 m2/g
- Electrical conductivity: 10-100 S/cm

Dual-Path Ion Ingress: The toroidal scaffold geometry creates two simultaneous
ion ingress pathways:
- Center Path: Al3+ ions enter through the central hollow of the torus,
migrating radially outward through the scaffold.
- Periphery Path: Al3+ ions enter from the outer surface of the torus,
migrating radially inward.
- Result: Every graphene surface within the scaffold is reachable from both
directions simultaneously. Maximum ion travel distance equals r/2 (half the
minor radius = 4mm), reduced further by scaffold porosity to effective distance
of approximately 50-200 micrometers.

Flash-Intercalation: The system achieves 0-100% charge in under 60 seconds
at the designed C-rate without lithium plating (no lithium in system),
electrolyte decomposition (ionic liquid stable above 4V), or capacity fade
from mechanical stress (graphene scaffold elastically accommodates Al3+
insertion).
