7. On or about April 30, 2024, Plaintiff entered into an auto loan with Bank of America, N.A. for a 2024 Jeep Wrangler, Account Number 63010066944180, principal balance approximately $49,333.47.

8. Defendant is currently reporting information on Account 63010066944180 to Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion.

10. This mailing was delivered to 100 N. Tryon Street, Charlotte, NC 28255. Article Number: 9589 0710 5270 2244 1851 51. Signed by: Lavoris Little. Return Receipt: 9590 9402 9465 5069 6188 87. Physical green card is in Plaintiff's possession.

12. A second certified mailing was delivered to Defendant's El Paso, TX servicing center on approximately February 18, 2026 (Tracking: 9589071052702244185069). Defendant provided no response.

13. On March 12, 2026, Plaintiff served Defendant with a Second Notice of Default of Response via USPS Certified Mail, citing TILA § 1601, FCRA § 623, and FCRA § 611. A formal complaint was simultaneously filed with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau and the Georgia Attorney General Consumer Protection Division. The Georgia Attorney General complaint was received and signed by C.A. Tillman on March 20, 2026.

This Notice cited 15 U.S.C. § 1681n willful violation and issued a final demand requiring written response no later than May 9, 2026.

16. As of May 10, 2026, Defendant has provided zero response to any of the four certified mailings spanning February 4, 2026 through April 9, 2026. Three separate statutory response deadlines have elapsed — March 10, April 17, and May 9, 2026 — with no response from Defendant.

17. Defendant has continued to furnish information regarding Account 63010066944180 to consumer reporting agencies throughout this period without the required disputed notation.


COUNT I — WILLFUL VIOLATION OF 15 U.S.C. § 1681n
Fair Credit Reporting Act

18. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

19. Defendant received Plaintiff's formal written dispute. Three response deadlines — March 10, April 17, and May 9, 2026 — elapsed with no response.

20. Defendant's failure to respond was willful within the meaning of 15 U.S.C. § 1681n, entitling Plaintiff to statutory damages of $100 to $1,000 per violation, actual damages, punitive damages, and attorney fees.


COUNT II — WILLFUL VIOLATION OF 15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2(b)
Failure to Investigate as Furnisher

21. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

22. As a furnisher of information, Defendant was required upon receipt of dispute notice to conduct a reasonable investigation, review relevant information, report results to credit reporting agencies, and modify or delete inaccurate information.

23. Defendant failed to take any required action and continued reporting Account 63010066944180 without the disputed notation, in willful violation of 15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2(b).


COUNT III — VIOLATION OF 15 U.S.C. § 1601 et seq.
Truth in Lending Act

24. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

25. Plaintiff's February 4, 2026 Notice demanded: (a) the original retail installment contract; and (b) the Certificate of Title reflecting the exact lien holder entry — both required under TILA.

26. Defendant received this demand, its employee signed for it, and Defendant failed to produce any requested documentation. Defendant is exposed to civil liability under 15 U.S.C. § 1640(a) including actual and statutory damages up to $4,000.


COUNT IV — VIOLATION OF O.C.G.A. § 10-1-393
Georgia Fair Business Practices Act

27. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

28. Defendant's conduct — accepting four certified mailings over three months, having employees sign for each, maintaining complete silence across three statutory deadlines, and continuing to report an unvalidated account as delinquent to consumer reporting agencies affecting a Georgia resident — constitutes an unfair and deceptive act in the conduct of consumer transactions in violation of O.C.G.A. § 10-1-393.

29. Defendant's conduct was willful under O.C.G.A. § 10-1-399, entitling Plaintiff to actual damages, treble damages, and attorney fees.


COUNT V — DEFAMATION — LIBEL PER SE

30. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

31. Defendant published information to Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion characterizing Account 63010066944180 as a delinquent obligation after receiving formal written notice of dispute and after Defendant's own employee signed confirming receipt.

32. Publication of unvalidated adverse credit information without the required dispute notation, after confirmed receipt of formal dispute, constitutes libel per se under Georgia law.

33. Defendant acted with actual malice by continuing adverse reporting after receiving four certified notices over three months. Plaintiff is entitled to actual damages, presumed damages, and punitive damages.
