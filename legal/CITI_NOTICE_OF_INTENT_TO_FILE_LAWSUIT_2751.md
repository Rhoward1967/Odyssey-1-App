## PART II — DOCUMENTED VIOLATIONS NOW IN EFFECT

Independent monitoring of credit files maintained by Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion reveals that Citibank, N.A. is continuing to report Account 2751 as delinquent **WITHOUT** the mandatory **"Disputed by Consumer"** notation required by federal law upon receipt of a formal written dispute.

This constitutes active, ongoing, willful violations of federal and Georgia law:

### Violation 1 — 15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2(a)(3)
**Duty of Furnisher Upon Notice of Dispute**

A person who furnishes information to a consumer reporting agency shall notify the consumer reporting agency of any dispute with regard to the completeness or accuracy of any information being reported. Upon receipt of a written dispute from a consumer, a furnisher **must** update its reporting to reflect the disputed status. Citibank's continued reporting without the "Disputed" notation after receiving the March 30, 2026 Notice constitutes a direct violation of this statute.

**Exposure: Civil liability under 15 U.S.C. § 1681n — up to $1,000 statutory damages per violation per bureau, plus actual damages, plus attorney fees.**

### Violation 2 — 15 U.S.C. § 1692e(8)
**Prohibition on False Credit Reporting**

It is a violation of the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act to communicate credit information which is known or which should be known to be false, including the **failure to communicate that a disputed debt is disputed**. Having received formal written notice of dispute on March 30, 2026, any continued reporting that omits the disputed status constitutes a false communication under this statute.

**Exposure: Civil liability under 15 U.S.C. § 1692k — $1,000 statutory damages per violation, plus actual damages, plus attorney fees.**

### Violation 3 — O.C.G.A. § 10-1-393
**Georgia Fair Business Practices Act — Deceptive Acts and Practices**

Continuing to report a debt as valid and undisputed to credit bureaus — after receiving a formal written legal challenge asserting documented grounds for invalidity — constitutes a deceptive act in the conduct of commerce under Georgia law. This applies independently of federal remedies and provides additional state court standing.

**Exposure: Treble damages for willful violations, plus attorney fees, under O.C.G.A. § 10-1-399.**

### Violation 4 — 15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2(b)
**Failure to Investigate Dispute**

Upon receiving notice of a consumer dispute, a furnisher must conduct a reasonable investigation and report the results to the credit bureau. No investigation response has been received. The failure to investigate within the statutory period constitutes an independent willful violation.

**Exposure: Civil liability under 15 U.S.C. § 1681n.**

Upon expiration of the 72-hour cure period without full compliance, the **Howard Jones Bloodline Ancestral Trust** and **Rickey Allan Howard** will initiate civil action in the **Superior Court of Clarke County, Georgia**, seeking the following relief:

### Statutory Damages
- **$1,000 per violation per credit bureau** under 15 U.S.C. § 1681n (3 bureaus × violations = minimum $3,000)
- **$1,000 per FDCPA violation** under 15 U.S.C. § 1692k
- **Treble damages** under O.C.G.A. § 10-1-399 for willful Georgia Fair Business Practices Act violations

### Actual Damages
- Documented harm to creditworthiness and access to credit
- Defamation of credit — the continued reporting of a formally disputed account as undisputed constitutes publication of false information
- Economic harm arising from denial of credit opportunities during the dispute period
- Emotional distress damages cognizable under Georgia law

### Injunctive Relief
- Court order requiring immediate removal of the trade line from all three credit bureaus
- Court order prohibiting any further collection activity on Account 2751
- Court order requiring Citibank to respond fully to the March 30, 2026 Notice of Challenge

### Punitive Damages
- Willful violation of federal consumer protection statutes with knowledge of the disputed status warrants punitive damages under applicable federal and Georgia standards

### Attorney Fees and Costs
- Both 15 U.S.C. § 1681n and 15 U.S.C. § 1692k provide for mandatory attorney fee awards to prevailing plaintiffs in willful violation cases

### Additional Grounds
