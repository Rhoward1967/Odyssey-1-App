## TECHNICAL DESCRIPTION

The Locus Ring is a wearable titanium ring containing an 8-channel sub-muscular
neural sensor array that reads biological intent signals before they manifest as
physical movement. It serves as the biological input interface for the entire
R.O.M.A.N. 2.0 system.

### Core Innovation: Reading Intent Before Movement

Standard input devices (keyboard, mouse, touchscreen) capture movement after it
occurs — they read the result of a decision. The Locus Ring reads the neural signal
that causes movement before the muscle fires. This is the hardware basis of the
NPU Intent Translation Layer (Innovation #10).

**Signal pathway:**
```
User forms intent
      ↓
Sub-muscular nerve impulse fires (before muscle movement)
      ↓
8-channel sensor array captures impulse (Locus Ring)
      ↓
Lumen-Link transmits raw bio-signal to R.O.M.A.N. SoC
      ↓
NPU translates bio-signal into schema-compliant RomanCommand
      ↓
Command enters Dual-Hemisphere validation pipeline
```

### 8-Channel Sensor Array

Eight independent neural sensor channels are distributed around the inner
circumference of the ring. Multi-channel design provides:
- Redundancy: signal loss in one channel does not interrupt operation
- Precision: cross-referencing 8 channels filters noise from genuine intent signals
- Differentiation: distinct gesture vocabulary mapped to specific R.O.M.A.N. actions

### Self-Powered: Thermoelectric Generator

The Locus Ring requires no battery and no charging. A **thermoelectric generator
(TEG)** converts the differential between body temperature (98.6°F) and ambient
air into continuous electrical power. As long as the ring is worn, it is powered.

### Lumen-Link Passive Power Reception

When within range of the Lumen Core, the Locus Ring also receives power passively
via the Lumen-Link Protocol's 15W wireless coupling — supplementing the TEG and
ensuring full-power operation during active compute sessions.

### Physical Design

- **Material:** Titanium (biocompatible, non-conductive housing)
- **Form:** Torus geometry — consistent inner contact with finger tissue for
  reliable sensor contact
- **Sizing:** Configurable (no active electronics in sizing band — sensors
  concentrated in the primary ring body)
- **Weight:** Minimized by hollow titanium construction with sensor array
  integrated into inner surface
