                      ARTICLE III - MANAGEMENT AND CONTROL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

3.1  MEMBER-MANAGED
     The Company is member-managed. The Member has full and complete authority
     to manage and control the business and affairs of the Company.

3.2  POWERS OF THE MEMBER
     The Member has full power and authority to:
     
     a) Enter into contracts and agreements on behalf of the Company;
     
     b) Hire employees, contractors, and consultants;
     
     c) Open bank accounts and manage Company finances;
     
     d) Incur debts and obligations on behalf of the Company;
     
     e) Purchase, lease, sell, or otherwise acquire or dispose of property;
     
     f) File, prosecute, maintain, license, and enforce patents, trademarks,
        copyrights, and other intellectual property;
     
     g) Raise capital through debt or equity financing;
     
     h) Make distributions of Company profits;
     
     i) File tax returns and maintain books and records;
     
     j) Engage attorneys, accountants, and other professionals;
     
     k) Take any and all actions necessary or appropriate to carry out the
        Company's business.

3.3  NO PERSONAL LIABILITY
     The Member shall not be personally liable for the debts, obligations, or
     liabilities of the Company, except as required by Georgia law or as the
     Member may personally guarantee in writing.

3.4  INDEMNIFICATION
     The Company shall indemnify and hold harmless the Member from any claims,
     liabilities, damages, or expenses arising from the Member's good faith
     actions on behalf of the Company, except for acts of gross negligence,
     willful misconduct, or fraud.

3.5  STANDARD OF CARE
     The Member shall manage the Company in good faith and in a manner
     reasonably believed to be in the best interests of the Company.

     The Member hereby confirms and agrees that all intellectual property

     Company in writing, and that the Company is the sole owner of such

     All works created by the Member or by employees, contractors, or agents
     of the Company within the scope of their engagement shall be deemed "works
     made for hire" and shall be owned by the Company.

4.4  FURTHER ASSURANCES

     assignments, trademark registrations, and copyright filings.

4.5  CONFIDENTIALITY

     secrets and proprietary information, both during and after membership in

                    ARTICLE V - FINANCIAL MATTERS AND ACCOUNTING
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

5.1  FISCAL YEAR
     The fiscal year of the Company shall be the calendar year ending
     December 31.

5.2  BOOKS AND RECORDS
     The Company shall maintain complete and accurate books and records of its
     business and financial affairs at the principal office, including:
     
     a) A copy of this Operating Agreement and all amendments
     b) A copy of the Articles of Organization and all amendments
     c) Federal and state tax returns and financial statements
     d) Records of capital contributions and distributions
     e) Minutes of any meetings or written consents
     f) Contracts, agreements, and other material documents

5.3  BANK ACCOUNTS
     The Member shall open one or more bank accounts in the Company's name and
     shall ensure that all Company funds are deposited in such accounts. The
     Member is authorized to sign checks, make withdrawals, and otherwise
     transact business on behalf of the Company.

5.4  SEPARATE FUNDS
     The Company's funds and assets shall be kept separate from the personal
     funds and assets of the Member. The Member shall not commingle personal
     and Company funds.

5.5  ACCOUNTING METHOD
     The Company shall use the accrual method of accounting unless otherwise
     determined by the Member or required by law.

5.6  TAX MATTERS
     
     a) Tax Classification: As a single-member LLC, the Company shall be
        treated as a disregarded entity for federal income tax purposes unless
        the Member elects otherwise by filing Form 8832 or Form 2553.
     
     b) Tax Returns: The Member shall ensure that all required federal, state,
        and local tax returns are timely filed.
     
     c) Tax Compliance: The Company shall comply with all applicable tax laws
        and regulations.

                     ARTICLE VI - DISTRIBUTIONS AND ALLOCATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

6.1  DISTRIBUTIONS
     The Member may, at any time and in the Member's sole discretion, authorize
     distributions of Company profits or assets. All distributions shall be made
     to the Member as the sole owner of the Company.

6.2  NO OBLIGATION TO DISTRIBUTE
     The Member shall not be obligated to make distributions and may retain
     profits for working capital, growth, or other business purposes.

6.3  ALLOCATION OF PROFITS AND LOSSES
     All profits, losses, credits, and deductions of the Company shall be
     allocated 100% to the Member.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                        ARTICLE VII - DISSOLUTION AND WINDING UP
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

7.1  EVENTS OF DISSOLUTION
     The Company shall be dissolved upon:
     
     a) The written consent of the Member to dissolve the Company;
     
     b) The death, resignation, expulsion, bankruptcy, or dissolution of the
        Member, unless the business is continued in accordance with Georgia law;
     
     c) Entry of a decree of judicial dissolution;
     
     d) Any other event that makes it unlawful or impossible to continue the
        business.

7.2  WINDING UP
     Upon dissolution, the Member (or a court-appointed liquidator) shall wind
     up the Company's affairs by:
     
     a) Giving notice to creditors;
     
     b) Liquidating Company assets;
     
     c) Paying or providing for all Company debts and obligations;
     
     d) Distributing remaining assets to the Member.

7.3  CERTIFICATE OF CANCELLATION
     After winding up is complete, the Member shall file a Certificate of
     Cancellation with the Georgia Secretary of State.

7.4  NO DEFICIT RESTORATION
     The Member shall not be required to restore any deficit in the Member's
     capital account.
