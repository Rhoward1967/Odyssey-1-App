## FORUM ONE: THE INTER-AMERICAN COMMISSION ON HUMAN RIGHTS

**The Most Accessible International Forum**

The Inter-American Commission on Human Rights is an organ of the Organization of American States. It accepts individual petitions from persons whose rights under the American Convention on Human Rights or the American Declaration of the Rights and Duties of Man have been violated by a member state — including the United States.

### Who Can File

Any person, group of persons, or nongovernmental organization legally recognized in one or more member states may file a petition with the IACHR. You do not need an attorney. You do not need institutional affiliation. You need to be a living being whose rights were violated by the United States government and who has exhausted available domestic remedies.

### What the IACHR Can Review

The IACHR is not bound by the Supreme Court's procedural default doctrine. It is not bound by the harmless error rule. It is not bound by qualified immunity. It evaluates whether the living being's human rights were violated — period. The procedural barriers the domestic system used to prevent review of the violation do not follow the petition to the IACHR.

### The Deadline — Critical

**A petition must be filed within six months of the final domestic decision.** For Supreme Court cert denials, the clock starts running on the date of the cert denial. Six months. This is not extendable. Missing it forecloses the IACHR forum.

### What to Include in the Petition

- Full identification of the petitioner and the alleged victim
- Description of the facts — what happened, when, where, and who was responsible
- The rights violated — identified by reference to the American Convention or American Declaration provisions
- The domestic remedies pursued — every court, every filing, every denial
- Evidence that domestic remedies have been exhausted or are unavailable
- The specific relief requested — investigation, finding of violation, recommendation for remedy

The IACHR petition process is one of the most powerful tools available to the living being after domestic exhaustion. The United States participates in the OAS system. IACHR findings against the United States are public, reported to Congress, and create international accountability that the domestic cert denial does not.

**FINDING:** The IACHR has documented systematic human rights violations against African Americans and indigenous peoples in the United States across multiple reports. The living being who files a well-documented petition is not making a novel claim — they are adding their specific documented experience to a pattern the IACHR already recognizes.

## FORUM TWO: THE UN HUMAN RIGHTS COMMITTEE

**Individual Communications Under the ICCPR Optional Protocol**

The United Nations Human Rights Committee monitors compliance with the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights. Under the Optional Protocol to the ICCPR, the Committee accepts individual communications from persons who claim their ICCPR rights have been violated by a state party. The United States has ratified the ICCPR.

### The Deadline

Communications should be submitted as soon as possible after domestic remedies are exhausted. There is no strict six-month deadline as under the IACHR rules, but unreasonable delay can affect admissibility. File promptly.

### ICCPR Rights Most Relevant to the Living Being

- **Article 2:** right to effective remedy for rights violations
- **Article 6:** right to life — relevant to excessive force claims
- **Article 7:** prohibition on cruel, inhuman, or degrading treatment
- **Article 9:** right to liberty and security — unlawful detention
- **Article 14:** right to fair trial and effective appeal
- **Article 17:** protection against arbitrary interference with privacy
- **Article 26:** equal and effective protection against discrimination
- **Article 27:** right to enjoy one's own culture — indigenous and African ancestral cultural rights

The Human Rights Committee's views on individual communications are not legally binding in the same way as domestic court judgments. But they are authoritative interpretations of international human rights law, published in the Committee's annual report to the UN General Assembly, and cited by courts and human rights bodies worldwide. A favorable Committee view creates international accountability and strengthens subsequent domestic claims.

## FORUM THREE: UN SPECIAL RAPPORTEURS

**Communications to Independent UN Experts**

The United Nations Human Rights Council appoints Special Rapporteurs as independent experts to investigate and report on specific human rights issues worldwide. Special Rapporteurs accept individual communications about alleged violations within their mandate. They can send urgent appeals to governments and include cases in their annual reports to the Human Rights Council.

### Special Rapporteurs Most Relevant to the Living Being's Claims

- **Special Rapporteur on Contemporary Forms of Racism, Racial Discrimination, Xenophobia and Related Intolerance** — for racial discrimination, discriminatory enforcement, and the ongoing badges and incidents of slavery
- **Special Rapporteur on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples** — for Creek dispossession, ancestral land claims, and cultural rights violations
- **Special Rapporteur on Adequate Housing** — for redlining, discriminatory appraisal, and housing discrimination claims
- **Special Rapporteur on the Promotion and Protection of Human Rights While Countering Terrorism** — for overreach of law enforcement powers
- **Special Rapporteur on Torture and Other Cruel, Inhuman or Degrading Treatment or Punishment** — for excessive force claims
- **Special Rapporteur on the Independence of Judges and Lawyers** — for the commercial tribunal and impartiality violations documented in the Founding Crime Treatise

There is no strict deadline for Special Rapporteur communications. File when the violation is documented and the domestic process has been completed or is demonstrably inadequate.

## FORUM FOUR: UN WORKING GROUP OF EXPERTS ON PEOPLE OF AFRICAN DESCENT

The UN Working Group of Experts on People of African Descent was established by the Human Rights Council to study the problems of racial discrimination faced by people of African descent and to propose measures to eliminate racial discrimination. The Working Group has conducted country visits to the United States and produced comprehensive reports documenting systematic violations against African Americans including mass incarceration, police violence, health disparities, and the ongoing economic legacy of slavery.

The Working Group accepts individual communications and considers them in the context of its broader country monitoring. Its 2016 report on the United States — A/HRC/33/61 — is the most comprehensive international human rights assessment of the condition of African Americans ever produced by a UN body. Filing a communication with the Working Group adds the living being's specific documented experience to the pattern the Working Group has already formally recognized.

## FORUM FIVE: THE COMMITTEE ON THE ELIMINATION OF RACIAL DISCRIMINATION

The Committee on the Elimination of Racial Discrimination monitors implementation of the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination. The United States has ratified ICERD. The Committee reviews state party reports and issues concluding observations. It also accepts inter-state communications and, under Article 14, individual communications from persons claiming ICERD rights violations.

ICERD's definition of racial discrimination — any distinction, exclusion, restriction, or preference based on race that has the **purpose or effect** of impairing the enjoyment of rights — is an effect-based standard that does not require proof of discriminatory intent. This is the standard the domestic system's *Washington v. Davis* intent requirement prevents domestic courts from applying. The CERD provides the effect-based forum the domestic system deliberately closed.
