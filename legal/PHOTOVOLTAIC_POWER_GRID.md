## TECHNICAL DESCRIPTION

The Photovoltaic Power Grid is the primary energy harvesting system for the Lumen Core.
It replaces the single-point-of-failure Power Supply Unit found in traditional computing
with an isotropic, decentralized energy collection network distributed across the
geometric vertices of the 3×3×3 cubic architecture.

**Core Principle:** In conventional systems, power is a dependency — the machine is
inert without an external supply. In this architecture, power is a **Constitutional
Characteristic** — the system's geometry is its metabolism.

---

### 1. Geometric Distribution — The 8-Vertex Grid

The grid consists of 8 identical modules located at the absolute corners (vertices)
of the 3×3×3 Lumen Core. The 8 vertices of a cube are the only positions that are
simultaneously:
- Equidistant from the center SoC
- Exposed on three faces simultaneously (maximum light-harvesting surface area)
- Geometrically symmetric (isotropic distribution)

**Isotropic Harvesting:** Because modules occupy all 8 vertices, the system harvests
energy from any angle of incidence. No matter the Core's physical orientation, at
least 4 corner modules present an optimal face to any ambient light source.

**Parallel-Sovereign Redundancy:** The 8 modules operate in a parallel configuration.
If 1 to 7 modules are shaded, damaged, or removed during hot-swap, the remaining
modules maintain continuous power to the R.O.M.A.N. SoC without interruption.

---

### 2. Full-Spectrum Harvesting

Unlike standard photovoltaic panels optimized for direct solar irradiance, the
corner modules use multi-junction cells designed for full-spectrum operation:

- **Ambient indoor light:** Optimized for low-lux environments (300–500 lux
  typical office/home lighting) — the system operates without sunlight
- **Direct solar:** High-efficiency conversion for outdoor deployment
- **Thermal-to-electric layer:** Thin-film thermoelectric layer integrated
  into each module captures ambient heat differential and converts it to
  supplemental power — architecturally consistent with the Vapor-Chamber
  Cooling system (Innovation #18) which also operates on thermal differential

---

### 3. Hot-Swappable Energy Modules

Each of the 8 corner modules contains an integrated solid-state buffer capacitor.
This enables the **100% Uptime Guarantee** during Perpetual Incremental Renewal:
a power module can be physically removed and replaced while the system is running
because the remaining 7 modules and their buffers maintain the SoC's power floor
throughout the swap window.

The Lumen-Link Protocol (Innovation #17) carries power distribution signals between
modules, allowing the SoC to dynamically re-balance load across available corner
modules in real time.

## NOVELTY STATEMENT

Traditional compute hardware uses a **Linear Power Chain**:
`Grid → PSU → Motherboard`. One point of failure stops the entire system.

The Photovoltaic Power Grid is the first known **Isotropic Power Mesh** for
AI compute hardware where:

1. **Energy is harvested at the vertices** of the compute volume, not delivered
   to a central port — the geometry itself determines the power topology
2. **Power is decentralized** across 8 independent nodes — no PSU, no single
   failure point
3. **The system is ambient-first** — designed to operate indefinitely on the
   light and heat already present in the environment without a tethered connection
4. **Thermal recovery is closed-loop** — heat generated by compute is partially
   recaptured by the same modules managing cooling, reducing net energy draw
