## Complaint Title
TransUnion — Willful Evasion of FCRA Dispute Obligations via Undeliverable Address

---

## Narrative

On or about February 9, 2026, I mailed a formal written dispute via USPS Certified Mail (Tracking #: 9589 0710 5270 2244 1697 79) to TransUnion, PO Box 2000, Chester, PA 19016 — the address listed on TransUnion's own website for consumer disputes. The USPS returned the envelope marked **"Return to Sender — Illegible / Unable to Forward,"** constituting documented evidence that TransUnion is maintaining a non-functional address for USPS-delivered consumer disputes.

### The Disputed Account

**Capital One Platinum MasterCard — Account Ending in 8746**

This is a single account appearing on both credit files. The same account number (8746) is being reported with materially contradictory information:

| Data Field | Equifax Reporting | TransUnion Reporting |
|---|---|---|
| **Account Status** | 90 Days Past Due | Current / Paid as Agreed |
| **Current Balance** | $20,329.00 | $5,171.00 |
| **Last Payment** | December 2020 | Recent (2025/2026) |
| **Credit Limit** | Not Reported / $0 | $5,300.00 |
| **Discrepancy** | — | **$15,158.00** |

### Mathematical Impossibility

Equifax is reporting a balance of **$20,329.00** on an account with a verified credit limit of **$5,300.00**. This represents a balance that is **383% of the credit limit** — a mathematical impossibility for a standard revolving credit card account under normal reporting standards. No legitimate combination of purchases, interest, and fees on a $5,300 limit card produces a $20,329 balance without systematic data corruption or a mixed-file error.

TransUnion, by contrast, reports a $5,171 balance — a figure that is consistent with the $5,300 credit limit. **Both cannot be accurate simultaneously.**

This is not a borderline discrepancy — this is a demonstrably false entry on Equifax's file that TransUnion has failed to reconcile despite the bureaus' obligations to share and verify dispute information under FCRA §611(d).

### TransUnion's Evasion

TransUnion has made it physically impossible for me to exercise my statutory right to dispute under the FCRA by maintaining a non-functional, USPS-undeliverable address for consumer disputes. This constitutes:

1. **Willful non-compliance** under FCRA §616
2. **Failure to maintain reasonable procedures** under FCRA §607(b)
3. **Bad faith obstruction** of the consumer dispute process
4. **Denial of statutory dispute rights** under FCRA §611(a)(1)
5. **Failure to synchronize** cross-bureau discrepancy data after receipt of dispute notice

## Relief Requested

I am requesting the CFPB take immediate enforcement action and order TransUnion to:

1. **Delete** the Capital One (acct. ending 8746) tradeline immediately from TransUnion's file, due to their inability to receive and investigate the dispute
2. **Investigate and correct** the cross-bureau discrepancy with Equifax per FCRA §611(d)
3. **Provide** a functional, USPS-deliverable address for consumer disputes within 15 days
4. **Pay** statutory damages under FCRA §616 ($100–$1,000 per violation)
5. **Notify** all bureaus of any corrections per FCRA §611(d)

Additionally, I request the CFPB investigate **Equifax** separately for reporting a $20,329 balance on an account with a $5,300 credit limit — a figure that is mathematically impossible and constitutes a per se violation of FCRA §607(b)'s requirement to maintain reasonable procedures for maximum possible accuracy.

## Legal Basis

- **FCRA §611(a)(1)** — Failure to conduct reasonable reinvestigation
- **FCRA §611(d)** — Failure to notify other bureaus of corrections
- **FCRA §607(b)** — Failure to maintain reasonable procedures for maximum possible accuracy
- **FCRA §616** — Willful noncompliance (statutory + punitive damages + attorney's fees)
- **FCRA §617** — Negligent noncompliance (actual damages + attorney's fees)
