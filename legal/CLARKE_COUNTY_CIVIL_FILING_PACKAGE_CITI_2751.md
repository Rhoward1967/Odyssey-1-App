## COMPLAINT FOR DAMAGES AND INJUNCTIVE RELIEF

---

### INTRODUCTION

Plaintiff Rickey Allan Howard brings this action against Citibank, N.A. for willful violations of the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA), and the Georgia Fair Business Practices Act, arising from Citibank's continued reporting of a formally disputed account as undisputed — in direct violation of federal and state law — after receiving formal written notice of dispute.

---

### PARTIES

**1.** Plaintiff Rickey Allan Howard is a natural person domiciled in Clarke County, Georgia, at 595 Macon Hwy, Apt 35, Athens, Georgia 30606. Plaintiff is a "consumer" as defined by 15 U.S.C. § 1681a(c) and 15 U.S.C. § 1692a(3).

**2.** Plaintiff Howard Jones Bloodline Ancestral Trust (EIN: 41-6850149) is an irrevocable trust organized under the laws of the State of Georgia, with its administrative record filed in Clarke County, Georgia (Public Record Filing ID: 15152515). Rickey Allan Howard is the Grantor-Trustee.

**3.** Defendant Citibank, N.A. is a national banking association with its principal place of business at 5800 South Corporate Place, Sioux Falls, SD 57108. Citibank is a "furnisher of information" as defined under 15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2 and regularly furnishes consumer credit information to Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. Citibank may be served through its Georgia registered agent: CT Corporation System, 289 S. Culver Street, Lawrenceville, GA 30046.

---

### JURISDICTION AND VENUE

**4.** This Court has subject matter jurisdiction over Plaintiff's Georgia state law claims under O.C.G.A. § 10-1-393 and concurrent jurisdiction over federal claims under 15 U.S.C. § 1681p and 15 U.S.C. § 1692k.

**5.** Venue is proper in Clarke County, Georgia under O.C.G.A. § 9-10-93 because Plaintiff resides in Clarke County, the harm was suffered in Clarke County, and Plaintiff's public record filings establishing constructive notice are recorded in Clarke County.

**6.** This Court has personal jurisdiction over Citibank, N.A. under Georgia's long-arm statute, O.C.G.A. § 9-10-91, because Citibank regularly conducts business with Georgia residents and committed tortious acts against a Georgia resident within this state.

---

### FACTUAL BACKGROUND

**7.** On or about February 29, 2024, Plaintiff opened a Citi Diamond Preferred credit card account, Account Number ending in 2751, with an original approved credit limit of $3,000.

**8.** Citibank subsequently increased the credit limit to $6,700 without Plaintiff's documented written consent to a new credit agreement.

**9.** Citibank unilaterally imposed a Penalty APR of 28.74% effective February 17, 2026, and increased the Cash Advance APR to 29.99% effective January 28, 2026, without mutual agreement.

**10.** On March 19, 2026, Citibank mailed a purported "debt validation" response claiming the alleged balance of $6,870.91 was valid and stating it "considered the matter addressed."

**11.** On March 30, 2026, Plaintiff served Citibank via United States Certified Mail with a **Formal Notice of Challenge to Debt Validity** (Reference: HJBAT-CITI-2751-CHALLENGE-033026), formally disputing the debt on six independent legal grounds including failure of lawful consideration, unilateral contract modification, and constitutional monetary defects.

**12.** The March 30, 2026 Notice constituted formal written dispute notice under 15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2(a)(3) and 15 U.S.C. § 1692e(8).

**13.** Upon information and belief, following receipt of the March 30, 2026 Notice, Citibank continued to report Account 2751 to Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion as delinquent **without** the required "Disputed by Consumer" notation.

**14.** Citibank failed to conduct a reasonable investigation and failed to report the results of any investigation to the credit reporting agencies within the statutory period.

**15.** On April 15, 2026, Plaintiff served Citibank with a Notice of Intent to File Lawsuit (Reference: HJBAT-CITI-2751-LAWSUIT-NOTICE-041526) demanding cure within 72 hours.

**16.** Citibank failed to cure the violations within 72 hours of confirmed receipt.

**17.** Citibank had constructive notice of Plaintiff's disputed status and Trust standing from the Clarke County public record filings (Filing ID: 15152515) predating any enforcement action.

---

### COUNT I
**Violation of 15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2(a)(3)**
**Failure to Provide Notice of Dispute to Credit Reporting Agencies**

**18.** Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

**19.** After receiving Plaintiff's written dispute on March 30, 2026, Citibank was required under 15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2(a)(3) to notify each consumer reporting agency to which it furnished information that the account was disputed.

**20.** Citibank willfully failed to update its reporting to reflect the disputed status.

**21.** This willful failure to comply with 15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2(a)(3) constitutes a violation entitling Plaintiff to statutory damages of up to $1,000 per violation under 15 U.S.C. § 1681n, plus actual damages, plus attorney fees and costs.

---

### COUNT II
**Violation of 15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2(b)**
**Failure to Investigate Consumer Dispute**

**22.** Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

**23.** After receiving notice of Plaintiff's dispute, Citibank was required to conduct a reasonable investigation into the disputed information and report the results to the credit reporting agencies.

**24.** Citibank failed to conduct a reasonable investigation and failed to correct, delete, or report inaccurate information as required.

**25.** This violation entitles Plaintiff to statutory damages under 15 U.S.C. § 1681n, actual damages, and attorney fees.

---

### COUNT III
**Violation of 15 U.S.C. § 1692e(8)**
**False and Misleading Credit Reporting**

**26.** Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

**27.** It is a violation of the FDCPA to communicate credit information which is known to be false, including the failure to communicate that a disputed debt is disputed.

**28.** After receiving Plaintiff's written dispute, Citibank communicated credit information to Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion without noting the disputed status — constituting a false and misleading representation under 15 U.S.C. § 1692e(8).

**29.** This violation entitles Plaintiff to statutory damages of $1,000 under 15 U.S.C. § 1692k, actual damages, and attorney fees.

---

### COUNT IV
**Violation of O.C.G.A. § 10-1-393**
**Georgia Fair Business Practices Act — Deceptive Acts and Practices**

**30.** Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

**31.** Citibank's continued reporting of a formally disputed account as undisputed — after receiving written notice of dispute and constructive notice through public record filings — constitutes an unfair and deceptive act in the conduct of trade or commerce in violation of O.C.G.A. § 10-1-393.

**32.** Citibank's conduct was willful within the meaning of O.C.G.A. § 10-1-399.

**33.** This violation entitles Plaintiff to actual damages, treble damages for willful violations, and attorney fees under O.C.G.A. § 10-1-399.

---

### COUNT V
**Defamation — Libel Per Se**

**34.** Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

**35.** Citibank published false statements of fact — specifically, that Plaintiff's Account 2751 was an undisputed delinquent debt — to third-party credit reporting agencies with knowledge that the account was formally disputed.

**36.** The publication of false credit information to consumer reporting agencies constitutes libel per se under Georgia law, as it directly injures Plaintiff in his trade, business, and financial reputation.

**37.** Citibank acted with actual malice — knowledge of falsity or reckless disregard for truth — by continuing to report without the disputed notation after receiving formal written notice.

**38.** Plaintiff is entitled to actual damages, presumed damages, and punitive damages for this willful defamatory conduct.

---
