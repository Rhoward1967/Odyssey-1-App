## SECTION ONE: THE FIRST HAND — The Etymology of Seizure

The word *emancipation* carries within its Latin syllables the entire history of what it was supposed to end.

**E-** (ex: out of / away from) — **MAN-** (manus: the Hand) — **CIP-** (capere: to seize) — **-ATIO** (the act of doing)

*Emancipation: the act of being taken out of the seizing hand.*

The Roman manus was not merely a metaphor for ownership. It was a specific legal institution — the formal power held by the paterfamilias over his wife, his children, and his slaves. To be in manu was to be in the hand: without independent legal standing, without the ability to own property, enter contracts, or appear in court except through the one whose hand held you.

The American slave system imported this structure — not just the practice of bondage but the specific Roman legal framework of the hand's ownership. The enslaved person was in manu. The admiralty courts that classified them as maritime cargo, the insurance documents that valued them as commercial property, the constitutional provisions that counted them as three-fifths of a person for apportionment while denying them any rights — all of this was the manus operating through American legal form.

When formal emancipation came, the living being was taken out of the seizing hand — e-manus-capere — but the hand remained. The hand that had classified them as property reclassified them as legal persons. The hand that had held the deed to their body now held the administrative construct associated with their name. The hand that had extracted their labor through the slave system now extracted it through the tax system, the debt system, the criminal justice system, and the regulatory apparatus.

The chain became lighter. The hand did not open.

**DOCTRINE FINDING:** The First Hand is the hand of the enterprise — the manus of Roman law inherited by the American slave system and continued through the administrative, commercial, and legal apparatus that reclassified the living being without releasing them. It is the seizing hand.
