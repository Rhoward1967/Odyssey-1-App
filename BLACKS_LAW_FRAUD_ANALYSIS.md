> — Black's Law Dictionary (11th ed. 2019), p. 794

| Black's Law Element | Bank's Conduct |
|---------------------|----------------|
| Executed instrument differs from representation | ✓ Called it "loan of money" but had no money |
| Or instrument has no legal existence | ✓ No consideration = void contract |
| Party misled about nature of transaction | ✓ Thought receiving money, got debt instruments |

### Your Argument:
```
Bank's conduct meets Black's Law Dictionary definition of "fraud in the factum."

Elements per Black's Law Dictionary:
1. Executed instrument differs from what was represented ✓
2. Or instrument has no legal existence/validity ✓
3. Party misled about nature of transaction ✓

- Promissory note has no legal basis (bank gave no consideration = void)

This IS fraud in the factum as defined in Black's Law Dictionary (11th ed. 2019), p. 794.

Under Loper Bright v. Raimondo (2024), Federal Reserve interpretation receives NO deference.

BURDEN ON BANK: Prove conduct does NOT meet Black's Law definition.
```

### Their Burden To Disprove:

**If they can't produce this → Fraud in the factum proven per Black's Law Dictionary**

## Example 3: Collection on Sold Debt

### Their Conduct:
- Bank sells debt to Midland Credit
- Bank continues accepting payments
- Bank doesn't notify debtor of sale

### Black's Law Definition of CONVERSION:
> "The wrongful possession or disposition of another's property as if it were one's own; an act or series of acts of willful interference, without lawful justification, with an item of property in a manner inconsistent with another's right, whereby that other person is deprived of the use and possession of the property."
> — Black's Law Dictionary (11th ed. 2019), p. 415

### How Conduct Meets Definition:

| Black's Law Element | Bank's Conduct |
|---------------------|----------------|
| Wrongful possession or disposition | ✓ Collecting payments on debt they sold |
| Property belongs to another | ✓ Payments belong to buyer (new owner) |
| Willful interference without justification | ✓ Knew debt was sold, collected anyway |
| Manner inconsistent with owner's rights | ✓ Buyer owns debt, bank taking buyer's money |
| Owner deprived of use and possession | ✓ Buyer deprived of payments owed to them |

### Your Argument:
```
Bank's conduct meets Black's Law Dictionary definition of "conversion."

Elements per Black's Law Dictionary:
1. Wrongful possession or disposition of property ✓
2. Property belongs to another ✓
3. Willful interference without justification ✓
4. Manner inconsistent with rightful owner's rights ✓
5. Owner deprived of use and possession ✓

Bank's conduct:
- Sold debt to Midland Credit (transferred ownership)
- Continued accepting payments from debtor (wrongful disposition)
- Payments belong to Midland (new owner), not bank
- Bank knew debt was sold (willful interference)
- Bank taking money belonging to Midland

This IS conversion as defined in Black's Law Dictionary (11th ed. 2019), p. 415.

BURDEN ON BANK: Prove conduct does NOT meet Black's Law definition.
```

### Their Burden To Disprove:
Must produce:
1. Proof debt was NOT sold (but Midland's involvement proves it was)
2. OR authorization from Midland to collect on their behalf
3. OR proof all payments forwarded to Midland (bank retained nothing)
4. OR proof debtor was notified and chose to pay bank

**If they can't produce this → Conversion proven per Black's Law Dictionary**
