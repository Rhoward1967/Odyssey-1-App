## Black's Law Dictionary Definitions

### 1. THEFT BY DECEPTION

**Definition:**
> "Theft committed by deceiving another into surrendering property or by obtaining property through a false representation of fact."
> — Black's Law Dictionary (11th ed. 2019), p. 1765

**Elements:**
1. ✓ Deceiving another person → Bank deceived you (said they loaned money)
2. ✓ False representation of fact → Bank represented having money to lend (false)
3. ✓ Obtaining property through deception → Bank got your property through foreclosure
4. ✓ Another surrenders property based on deception → You surrendered based on belief loan was valid

**Conclusion:** Bank's foreclosure IS theft by deception per Black's Law Dictionary.

### 2. LARCENY BY TRICK

**Definition:**

**Elements:**

### 3. FRAUD IN THE FACTUM

**Definition:**
> "Fraud occurring when a legal instrument as actually executed differs from the one intended for execution by the person who executes it, or when the instrument may have had no legal existence."

**Elements:**
